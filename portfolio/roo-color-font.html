Color and Typography Design System

Roopairs Product Design // Fall 2020

Color palette and typeface of the redefined design system.

Roopairs is a startup based in San Luis Obispo, building a service field management app for repair and maintenance companies. As their Product Design Intern, I had the opportunity to organize a design system with a focus on their font and color system.

The Problem and Solution

Our users were experiencing readability issues with the app. They often couldn't see the screen under outdoor glare, and had difficulties reading the smaller font sizes. With my co-intern Tyler, we expanded our knowledge through online research. We found that our typeface did not perform well on screen at smaller sizes, and our color system did not produce enough contrast.

We did some more online research to learn about some requirements to set for this project.

Here are the two requirements we set ourselves to accomplish:

  1. Generate a color system around the core brand color, fulfilling WCAG standards for color contrast.
  2. Pick a new typeface that matches the Roopairs brand and provides better performance at a low pixel-count.

How We Approached the Problem

Build a Design System and redefine Color and Typography. It will include:

  1. A documented color system to satisfy usability standards, to make the app more legible and usable.
  2. A documented type system to expand design possibilities and make the app more legible.

To accomplish this, we dove into research to have a better understanding about color, typography, and design systems.

The Research

We found that:

  • A color palette should be built using pre-existing tools from Material Design and Paletton.
  • Color usages should be fully tested with color contrasts measured on Figma.
  • Color contrast should pass WCAG small text standards.
  • The documentation should be done on Figma along with building a color and typography library for the team.
  • The documentation should should resemble the level of detail shown in the Mailchimp UX doc.
