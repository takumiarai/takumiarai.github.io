Color and Typography Design System

Roopairs Product Design // Fall 2020

Color palette and typeface of the redefined design system.

Roopairs is a startup based in San Luis Obispo, innovating a minimal yet powerful service field management software for repair and maintenance companies. As their Product Design Intern, I had the opportunity to redefine their typeface and color palette.

Through interviews, we found that our users experienced readability issues with our app. With my co-intern Tyler, we did some research and investigated our UI to discover that the app's contrast levels did not pass the industry standards. For users to have a smooth experience, we need a more clearly readable color system, with focus on gray levels. They also struggled to see our smaller, 12 point fonts.

In my previous quarter, I updated and reorganized their prototype files on Adobe XD. Throughout this process, I found inconsistencies in their typography. Sizing and weights were slightly different, and it gave the app a weak sense of hierarchy.

The core of these design problems was the lack of a design system. The startup launched off the ground purely with the vision of the CEO and CTO, so they worked without a documented design system. Now that the software and company must expand, there was a need to start a well-documented design system.

I discussed this with my mentor and Roopairs CEO, David Batrolomucci, and he gave me the great opportunity to give a presentation addressing the problems and action plan. To summarize, the presentation looked like this:

The Problem

  1. Users struggled to see the screen under outdoor glare. Our color system does not produce enough contrast.
  2. Users struggled to read the smaller fonts, even indoors. Our typeface underperforms at low pixel-counts.
  3. The app's font does not have enough of a weight difference to effectively show hierarchy. It limits design possibilities.

We came up with a solution to solve these problems.

The Solution

Build a Design System and redefine Color and Typography. It will include:

  1. A color system to satisfy usability standards, making the app more readable and usable.
  2. A type system that will expand design possibilities and provide clarity to our interface.
