Brisk Mobile App Prototype

The Event

SLO Hacks 2020

A 36-hour hackathon organized by Cal Poly students, biggest in the central coast.

The Project

Our team participated in this hackathon to create a FinTech Mobile App that can help people manage their spendings easier and faster. To accomplish this goal, we created a high-fidelity prototype on Figma and a feature implementation written in Python.

Initial Planning

To compete with the limited time we had, we quickly hashed out our initial direction and user interface ideas.

Research Survey

Through a survey at the hackathon, we found that half of the pool did not manage their spendings. They feel that it takes up too much time out of their day. They need a popular financial management that is visually appealing, fast, and effective.

Persona Development

We then created two personas to keep our vision in focus. This helped us solidify our idea of creating a simple, visually appealing, fast mobile app. Hence, we named it brisk. We also created two scenarios to metally visualize the app.

Competitive Analysis

We found that the best financial management apps have an appealing user interface and other features to automate the financial management. Those features connecting bank accounts and using geolocations. However, we thought those features were too invasive on the users' personal information. We kept our focus on creating an app that involves local input, synced to their personal Google Spreadsheet.

Goal

To create a financial management app with receipt scanning and Google Spreadsheets syncing capability.

Features

  1. Receipt Scanner

    Our programmers implemented our receipt scanning function using the Google Vision API. With this we were were able to read the store name and total price. We built a library for the scanner to categorize the purchase into a category. We would want to improve this in the future using a pre-existing database of store names. Unfortunately, we were not able to read the individual item names and prices due to the varying formatting of different receipts.

  2. Spreadsheet Sync

    Using the Google Sheets API, we were able to gather the data into a personal spreadsheet. Since users will log into BRISK using their Google account, the app can automatically sync the data with a spreadsheet. With this feature, users can safely manipulate their logged data however they want.

  3. Budget Bars

    This feature shown in our Figma high-fidelity prototype is for users to easily keep track of spendings per timeframe. At a glance they are able to see how much of their pre-determined budget they have spent so far. They have no need to do any of the number-crunching; all you need to do is open the app and take a quick look!

Design Iterations

We started our Figma prototype under the name of "Brisk", with the idea of having a bright blue gradient glowing in a dark-mode-like UI. At this stage, we implemented the wireframing and figured out the general flow of the app. As we kept building the prototype, we found that it did not look good with the screen shape of the iPhone X. Then, we decided to rebrand it as "Haya", with a brighter interface, with more rounded designs.

The Award

Best Image Processing and Object Detection Hack

After an allnighter reworking the figma prototype, we barely made it in time to pitch our product to the judges. We pitched a quick overview with the problem statement, vision statement, and design, followed by a demo of our implementation of the receipt scanning, spreadsheet syncing feature. We were selected as the "Best Image and Object Detection Hack"! I was so relieved to know that our hard work paid off. This was the best team I have ever worked with, and I'm grateful of the learning experience I had with my first ever Figma prototype.
